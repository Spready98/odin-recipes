A5-grade wagyu steak

The most coveted and expensive cut of steak is the Japanese A5 wagyu. The main defining characteristic of a wagyu steak in general is the marbling. Due to the high fat content (marbling) of a wagyu steak, it must be prepared in a unique way compared to a standard grocery store steak. However, just cause a wagyu steak needs to be prepared differently, does not mean it is neccessarily harder to prepare. In actuality, it is simpler to cook a wagyu and requires less technical skill. The simplicity of the recipe allows the wagyu to take centre stage. Generally, keeping things simple with high quality ingredients is optimal to get the most out of the ingredient. For example, oil is not required for a A5 Wagyu steak because it already has such high relative fat content that will melt like butter.

ingredients:

  • A5 wagyu
  • salt
  • thats it...

Steps:

  1. Salt 1-3 hours before cooking
  2. Pre-heat skillet in oven
  3. Put skillet on high heat and sear steak on both sides straight from the fridge
  4. Cook steak for 3 min on first side, then 2 min on other side
  5. Let the steak rest, slice into small pieces, serve
